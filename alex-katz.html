Biography

Alex Katz was born in New York on July 24, 1927. Influential pop artist whose work was exhibited in New York, Dublin, Venice, and London, among other cities. His paintings were large and simple, but bold and colorful. He was inspired by Japanese artist Kitagawa Utamaro. He collaborated with poet John Ashbery on works such as “Fragment” in 1966 and “Coma Berenices” in 2005. He committed his life to art after studying at the Skowhegan School of Painting and Sculpture in Maine. He fell in love with Maine and would devote a large portion of his output to depicting the state’s landscapes.
