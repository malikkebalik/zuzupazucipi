Scottie Pippen | Education and Childhood

Life is not always easy, and Scottie Pippen was no exception. Scottie was born on September 25, 1965, in Hamburg, Arkansas, to Ethel and Preston Pippen. He is the eldest of their twelve children.

Scottie did not grow up wealthy or in such a lavish home as he now has. The Pippens grew up in a two-room house in a 3,500-person town. His father worked in a paper mill factory, and the family was financially strapped.

Scottie’s parents were unable to afford college tuition for his children. Things became significantly more difficult during his freshman year when his father suffered a stroke. He was forced to resign from his job after being placed in a wheelchair until his death. Not long after this tragedy, his older brother Ronnie was also injured and forced to use a wheelchair.

Scottie’s only chance at a college education was through a scholarship. He attended Hamburg High School and was a standout basketball player on his team. Scottie was a point guard who led his team to the state playoffs, but colleges were uninterested in offering him a scholarship.

Scottie Pippen | University

After receiving no scholarship offers, his high school coach contacted a friend at the University of Central Arkansas, and he was offered the Basic Education Opportunity Grant.

With few options available to him, he enrolled at the University of Central Arkansas. Initially, he served as the team’s equipment manager due to the coach’s belief that he lacked the necessary upper body strength. It was only when players left the team that he seized the opportunity to fill the resulting vacancies.

Surprisingly, Pippen developed upper body strength and increased his height from 6’1 to 6’6′ before his sophomore year.

He became the best player in his sophomore year as a result of his new strength and experiences as a point guard. Not only did his average point increase, but so did his height, which increased to 6’8′. He appeared in 93 games during his four years of college, averaging 17.2 points per game.

Scottie Pippen | Professional Career

Bulls of Chicago (1987-1990)

Pippen was drafted by the Seattle SuperSonics in 1987 and was later acquired by the Chicago Bulls. He was overshadowed by Michael Jordan when he joined the Bulls. Jordan was already a member of the All-Star team.

Jordan and his coach Phil Jackson mentored him as he honed his existing skills and developed a new one. Pippen made his NBA-All Star debut in 1990.

Following Jordan’s initial abrupt retirement in 1993-1994, Pippen led the Bulls to a 55-game winning streak. He demonstrated to the world that he is more than a sidekick and earned the public’s attention. He won the Most Valuable Player Award and was named to his first of three consecutive All-NBA First Teams.

Team of Dreams (1992)

Pippen was a member of the 1992 United States men’s Olympic basketball team, the “Dream Team.” It was the first time an American Olympic team included active NBA players.

Rockets de Houston (1998-1999)

He was traded to the Houston Rockets in exchange for Roy Royers after 11 seasons with the Bulls. He was joined by Charles Barkley and Hakeem Olajuwon, two of his former Olympic teammates. During his tenure with the Rockets, he averaged 14.5 points per game, his lowest mark since his rookie season.

Trail Blazers of Portland (1999-2003)

Scottie made his NBA debut with the Portland Trail Blazers in 1999-2000. During the 1999-2000 season, while competing against the Bulls, he was honored with a video tribute highlighting his 11-year career, which Scottie described as”emotional.”

They were defeated in the Western Conference Finals by the Los Angeles Lakers, who later won the season.

He also played for the Blazers in 2000-01, appearing in 64 games. Regrettably, he missed 18 games as a result of his injury.

Additionally, he remained with the Portland Blazers for two additional seasons.

Bulls of Chicago (2003-2004)

Pippen returned to the Bulls after four seasons with the Portland Trail Blazers. He signed a two-year, $10 million contract. Despite his expertise and role as a mentor to new team members, he was injured numerous times. In 2004, the team missed the playoffs for the first time in his career. He announced his retirement from football in 2005 due to his injuries. Scottie appeared in 23 games during the 2003-04 season.

The Bulls retired his jersey number 33, making him the fourth player to hang from the rafters.

Scottie is currently ranked second in terms of minutes played and points scored for the 2019-2020 season.

Following Retirement

Although he retired in 2004, he returned to professional football in 2008 with Torpan Pojat and Sundsvall Dragons. He made a comeback at the age of 42 to compete in the Finnish League. He was compensated $66,000 by the Dragons for the games.

Pippen served as the Bulls’ team ambassador in 2010 and was named a senior advisor to the Bulls’ President and Chief Operating Officer in 2012.

Following his retirement, he has appeared in a number of television shows and films, including Midgets vs. Mascots, Fresh off the Boat, Chicago Fire, and The Last Dance.
