Alex Høgh Andersen is a Danish actor, filmmaker, and photographer, best known for his role as Ivar the Boneless in the action, adventure, drama TV series, Vikings, starting in 2016. He has also amassed a fanbase of more than 1.5 million followers on Instagram.

Education

Between the ages of 7 and 19, he attended a school for young children where he studied drama. Between 2006 and 2012, he was trained in drama at Eventyrteatret in Søborg, Gribskov, Denmark.

Alex attended the Københavns Universitet (University of Copenhagen) in Copenhagen, Denmark, where he pursued his degree in Film and Media Studies. However, he dropped out after only a year.

    Alex Høgh Andersen Facts

  • He was born and raised in Slagelse, Sjælland, a small town located southwest of Copenhagen, Denmark.
  • He started his acting career by appearing in various musical plays but decided he’d like to pursue a career in film at the age of 17.
  • His mom motivated him to start acting.
  • His 1st role was in 2010 as Arthur in the theater production of the play Sword in the Stone.
  • Alex was named “Upcoming TV-Star of the Year” for his role as William in the Christmas calendar show Tvillingerne og julemanden in 2013.
  • To perfect his craft, Alex has learned to sing, dance, fence, and perform stunts.
  • He considers himself a very visual-minded person and has thus developed a burning passion for photography.
  • Alex has tried his hand at creating and directing short films in the past. His dream is to become a director someday.
  • He originally didn’t audition for the role of Ivar the Boneless on Vikings but one of his brothers. However, as there were around 14 other guys auditioning, all good-looking and blue-eyed, the director suggested he try for Ivar.
  • Since his character was born with a brittle bone disease, he is constantly in pain and has trouble walking. To prepare, Alex practiced crawling and propping himself around the room for 3 weeks before the shooting started.
  • When asked to describe the scent of Travis Fimmel, who played the leading role of Ragnar Lothbrok on Vikings, Alex stated: “like flowers, cigarettes, sweat, and a hint of love.”
  • He described his accent in Vikings as “a Swedish accent with an Icelandic undertone and a little Finnish, but spoken in English.”
  • Alex is fluent in Danish and English and familiar with German, Swedish, and Norwegian.
  • As of February 2020, an actor he’d like to work with is Daniel Day-Lewis.
  • Alex does not believe heaven exists but if it did, he wishes it would have a basketball court and a bar.
  • His biggest turn-ons are talent, humor, laughter, nice smile, singing, and being good with kids.
  • His biggest turn-offs are when a person is violent, self-indulgent, disrespectful, acting dumb, and doesn’t brush their teeth.
  • Alex sings in the shower.
