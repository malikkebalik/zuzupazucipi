James Blunt is an English singer, songwriter, musician, and record producer who shot to acclaim in 2004 with the release of his debut album Back to Bedlam, achieving worldwide fame with the singles “You’re Beautiful” and “Goodbye My Lover”. The album became the best-selling album of the 2000s in the UK and is one of the best-selling albums in UK chart history. His discography also comprises of other albums and tracks such as All the Lost Souls, Some Kind of Trouble, Moon Landing, Once Upon a Mind, The Afterlove, 1973, Carry You Home, I Really Want You, Stay the Night, If Time Is All I Have, Bonfire Heart, When I Find Love Again, Heart to Heart, Love Me Better, Love Under Pressure, The Truth, Bartender, Cold, and Love, Love, Love. Moreover, James Blunt has received many awards including at least 2 Brit Awards, 2 MTV Video Music Awards, and 2 Ivor Novello Awards, as well as receiving at least 5 Grammy Award nominations and an Honorary Doctorate for Music in 2016 from the University of Bristol.
